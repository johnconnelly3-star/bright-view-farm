Our Story

Founded in 1975

A Philadelphia Businessman's Dream

Bright View Farm was founded in 1975 by John Connelly, a Philadelphia businessman with a deep love for the Thoroughbred. Connelly acquired 270 acres of prime pastureland in Chesterfield, New Jersey — a rural township in Burlington County long prized for its rolling fields and rich soil — and set about building a farm that would become one of the state's finest Thoroughbred nurseries.

From the start, the vision was clear: to breed and raise horses of the highest quality, with the patience and attention that only a dedicated family operation could provide. That vision has never wavered in the fifty years since.

The Land

A Farmhouse Built in 1736

The heart of Bright View Farm is a historic farmhouse built in 1736 — nearly three centuries old, and home today to Christine Connelly. This ancient dwelling grounds the farm in a sense of continuity and permanence that no amount of modern construction could replicate.

The 270 acres surrounding the farmhouse are carefully managed for the health of the horses that graze them. Pastures are tested annually for nutritional content and maintained to provide optimal forage throughout the seasons. Two barns and five large run-in sheds are positioned across the property, giving horses access to shelter without sacrificing the open turnout that young Thoroughbreds need to develop naturally.

Stewardship

Christine Connelly & the Next Chapter

Today, Bright View Farm is owned and operated by Christine Connelly, who has built upon her father's legacy while bringing her own expertise and vision to the operation. Under Christine's leadership, the farm has earned the TOBA New Jersey Breeder of the Year award four times — in 2011, 2022, 2023, and 2024 — a remarkable run of recognition that reflects the quality of every horse that leaves this farm.

In January 2026, that dedication reached its pinnacle when Book'em Danno, a horse Christine co-bred, was named the 2025 Eclipse Award Champion Male Sprinter — the highest individual honor in American Thoroughbred racing.

News & Accolades
Our Mission

One of New Jersey's Last Thoroughbred Nurseries

As Thoroughbred breeding operations have gradually disappeared from New Jersey over the decades, Bright View Farm has remained — a steadfast commitment to a tradition that the state once celebrated as an important part of its agricultural and sporting heritage.

We breed horses for both the racing circuit and the auction ring, taking pride in the individual development of each animal. Our clients are owners and breeders who understand that quality cannot be rushed — and who trust Bright View Farm to give their horses the time, space, and expertise they deserve.

That trust, earned over fifty years, is the foundation of everything we do.
